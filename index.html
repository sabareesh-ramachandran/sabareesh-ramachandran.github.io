About Me

I am an economist studying how governments in developing countries can improve outcomes for their citizens in sectors like education and health. I also explore the pathways out of poverty for the urban poor and the role of public programs in this.

Starting as Assistant Professor at Indian School of Business, Hyderabad in 2025.

Research

My research focuses on policy design and evaluation in areas like education, health, and social programs. Here are some highlights:

  • Using Household Rosters to Estimate Mortality During COVID in India: Published in the Journal of Development Economics, this work estimates excess deaths in India using household survey data.
  • Impact and Incidence of Housing Subsidies in Urban Slums: A study on India's flagship housing program and its effects on house quality and welfare.
  • Medical Colleges and Front-line Health Workers: Explores how medical colleges in India impact healthcare availability and outcomes in underserved areas.

Previous Ventures

I have contributed to various impactful projects through collaborations with institutions worldwide:

  • UC San Diego: Worked on randomized controlled trials and co-authored papers on public distribution systems and employment guarantees in India.
  • J-PAL South Asia: Conducted fieldwork for projects improving school governance and analyzing policy effectiveness.
  • IDFC Institute: Advised state governments in India during the COVID-19 pandemic, focusing on vaccination strategies and response analysis.
  • IISc and LSE: Developed research projects in public economics and theoretical computer science.
